History of Ross-Dowsley House

The Ross–Dowsley stone manor at 27 Buell Street was built in 1828 as a stately family dwelling for Mrs. Jane Ross, widow, along with her son John Ross, a merchant, and George Dowsley, a shoemaker. Recognized today as a designated heritage building, the manor has served many roles over the decades, including a butcher shop, pizza parlour, and several family-owned restaurants.

After standing vacant for several years, the building was purchased in 1998 by Marc Hardy and Linda Crossfield. They brought new life to the space by opening Buell St. Bistro, transforming it into a beloved local dining destination. Over the years, they expanded the restaurant to include an additional dining room with a cozy fireplace, a wheelchair-accessible entrance, and a charming courtyard patio.

In 2025, Samantha Taffner and Phil Berger became the new owners of Buell St. Bistro. With a deep respect for the building’s history and a passion for creative, seasonal food, they aim to carry the legacy forward, blending tradition with fresh ideas. The Bistro continues to serve the Brockville community as a gathering place where good food, warm atmosphere, and local flavor come together.
